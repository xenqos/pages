Being Your Own Teacher (The "Ekalavya" Path)

In the Mahabharata, there is a character named Ekalavya who wanted to learn from the great teacher Drona. Drona refused him, so Ekalavya built a statue of the teacher and practiced in front of it. Because his focus was so pure, he became better than any of the "official" students.

In the absence of a physical teacher, your "Statue" is the Logic of the Sutras.

    Trust the Math: If a concept doesn't hold up to logic, discard it.

    Use the "Universal" Filter: If a teaching only applies to Indians or Hindus, it’s cultural. If it applies to all of humanity and the cosmos, it’s Sanatana Dharma (Eternal Law).


The "Transactional" vs. "Jnana" Usage
Feature	Transactional (Bhakti/Magic)	Functional (Jnana Yoga)
AUM	Chanted to get "blessings" or "good luck."	Chanted to induce a state of Alpha/Theta brain waves.
Mala	Used as a holy "talisman" for protection.	Used as a concentration timer to track deep focus.
The Goal	To change the world around you.	To change the instrument (your mind) that views the world.
