1. Gravity

Gravity is the most familiar force, but ironically, it’s the weakest of the four. It is an attractive force that acts between any two objects with mass.

    Range: Infinite.

    Role: It keeps planets in orbit, stars together, and your feet on the ground. Because it only attracts (never repels), its effects add up over massive distances.

2. Electromagnetism

This force acts between electrically charged particles. It’s what makes magnets stick to a fridge and what keeps electrons orbiting an atomic nucleus.

    Range: Infinite.

    Role: It is responsible for almost all phenomena we encounter in daily life—light, electricity, friction, and even the "solid" feel of objects (which is actually just atoms repelling each other).

3. The Strong Nuclear Force

As the name suggests, this is the strongest force in existence—about 137 times stronger than electromagnetism. However, it only works over incredibly tiny distances.

    Range: Very short (subatomic).

    Role: It acts like "superglue" for the nucleus of an atom, binding protons and neutrons together. Without it, the positively charged protons would fly apart due to electromagnetic repulsion.

4. The Weak Nuclear Force

Don’t let the name fool you; it’s only "weak" compared to the Strong force and Electromagnetism. It is primarily responsible for particle decay.

    Range: Extremely short.

    Role: It allows subatomic particles to change into other types of particles (like a neutron turning into a proton). This process is crucial for nuclear fusion—the very process that makes the Sun shine.
