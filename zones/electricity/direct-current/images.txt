Without custom-sizing <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/video-lectures/electronic-components-resistors/" target="_blank">resistors</a> for each branch (to make resistances precisely equal between all the thermocouples), it is acceptable to simply install resistors with equal values, significantly higher than the thermocouple wires&rsquo; resistances so that those wire resistances will have a much smaller impact on the total branch resistance. These resistors are called <em>swamping</em> resistors because their relatively high values overshadow or &ldquo;swamp&rdquo; the resistance of the thermocouple wires themselves:<img src="/uploads/articles/peltier-effect.jpg" /></p>
