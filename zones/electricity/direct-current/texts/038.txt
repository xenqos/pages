# How to Calculate Power in a Series and Parallel Circuit
^[038-01.jpg]
<h3 class="text-lg lg:text-2xl font-bold mb-3">What is Electrical Power and How Can You Calculate it in Series and Parallel Circuits?</h3>
<p><p>Electrical power measures the rate of work represented in electrical circuits by the symbol &ldquo;P&rdquo; and the units of Watts (W). The total circuit power is additive for series, parallel, or any combination of series and parallel components.&nbsp;</p></p>
<p>When calculating the <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/textbook/experiments/chpt-2/power-dissipation/" target="_blank">power dissipation</a> of resistive components, we can use any one of the three <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/textbook/direct-current/chpt-2/voltage-current-resistance-relate/" target="_blank">Ohm&rsquo;s law</a> power equations if given any two of the <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/textbook/direct-current/chpt-1/voltage-current/" target="_blank">voltage (V), current (I)</a>, and <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/textbook/direct-current/chpt-1/resistance/" target="_blank">resistance (R)</a>:</p>

<p>$$P = IV = I^2R = \frac{V^2}{R}$$</p>

## Power in Series and Parallel Circuits
<p>Power is a measure of the rate of work. Per the physics law of conservation of energy, the power dissipated in the circuit must equal the total power applied by the source(s). Therefore, an interesting rule for total circuit power versus individual component power is that it is additive for any circuit configuration: series (Table 2), parallel (Table 3), or any combination of series and parallel.&nbsp;</p>

## Review of Power for Series and Parallel Circuits:
<ul>
<li>Electrical power is the measure of work</li>
<li>Power is represented by the symbol &ldquo;P&rdquo;&nbsp;</li>
<li>The unit of power is the Watt (W)</li>
<li>Power is additive in any configuration of a resistive circuit&mdash;series, parallel, or series-parallel</li>
<li>P<sub>total </sub>= P<sub>1</sub> + P<sub>2</sub> + . . . P<sub>n</sub></li>
</ul>
