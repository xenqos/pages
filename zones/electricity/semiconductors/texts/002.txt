# Active Versus Passive Devices
## Passive Devices
<p>Components incapable of controlling current by means of another electrical signal are called <em>passive</em> devices. <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/video-lectures/electronic-components-resistors/" target="_blank">Resistors</a>, <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/video-lectures/capacitors-part-1/" target="_blank">capacitors</a>, <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/video-lectures/inductors-part-1/" target="_blank">inductors</a>, <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/video-lectures/transformers-part-1/" target="_blank">transformers</a>, and even <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/video-lectures/diode-characteristics-circuits/" target="_blank">diodes</a> are all considered passive devices.</p>
## Active Devices
<p>An <em>active</em> device is any type of circuit component with the ability to electrically control electric charge flow (electricity controlling electricity). In order for a circuit to be properly called <em>electronic</em>, it must contain at least one active device. Active devices include, but are not limited to, <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/textbook/semiconductors/chpt-13/introduction-electron-tubes/" target="_blank">vacuum tubes</a>, <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/video-lectures/bipolar-transistors-intro/" target="_blank">transistors</a>, <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/textbook/semiconductors/chpt-7/silicon-controlled-rectifier-scr/" target="_blank">silicon-controlled rectifiers (SCRs)</a>, and <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/textbook/semiconductors/chpt-7/the-triac/" target="_blank">TRIACs</a>.</p>
<p>A case might be made for the saturable reactor to be defined as an active device, since it is able to control an <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/textbook/alternating-current/chpt-1/what-is-alternating-current-ac/" target="_blank">AC</a> current with a <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/textbook/direct-current/" target="_blank">DC</a> current, but I&rsquo;ve never heard it referred to as such. The operation of each of these active devices will be explored in later chapters of this volume.</p>
## Functions of Active Devices
<p>All active devices control the flow of electric charges through them. Some active devices allow a voltage to control this current while other active devices allow another current to do the job. Devices utilizing a static voltage as the controlling signal are, not surprisingly, called <em>voltage-controlled</em> devices.</p>
<p>Devices working on the principle of one current controlling another current are known as <em>current-controlled</em> devices.</p>
<p>For the record, vacuum tubes are voltage-controlled devices while transistors are made as either voltage-controlled or current controlled types.
