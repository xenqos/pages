# Band-pass Filters
## How to Create Band-pass Filter
<p>There are applications where a particular band, or spread, or frequencies need to be filtered from a wider range of mixed signals.&nbsp;Filter circuits can be designed to accomplish this task by combining the properties of low-pass and high-pass into a single filter. The result is called a <em>band-pass</em> filter.</p>

<p>The fact that the high-pass section comes &ldquo;first&rdquo; in this design instead of the low-pass section makes no difference in its overall operation. It will still filter out all frequencies too high or too low.</p>
<p>While the general idea of combining low-pass and high-pass filters together to make a bandpass filter is sound, it is not without certain limitations.</p>
<p>Because this type of band-pass filter works by relying on either section to <em>block</em> unwanted frequencies, it can be difficult to design such a filter to allow unhindered passage within the desired frequency range.</p>
<p>Both the low-pass and high-pass sections will always be blocking signals to some extent, and their combined effort makes for an attenuated (reduced amplitude) signal at best, even at the peak of the &ldquo;pass-band&rdquo; frequency range.</p>
<p>Notice the curve peak on the previous SPICE analysis: the load voltage of this filter never rises above 0.59 volts, although the source voltage is a full volt. This signal attenuation becomes more pronounced if the filter is designed to be more selective (steeper curve, narrower band of passable frequencies).</p>
<p>There are other methods to achieve band-pass operation without sacrificing signal strength within the pass-band. We will discuss those methods a little later in this chapter.</p>

<p><strong>REVIEW:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>A <em>band-pass</em> filter works to screen out frequencies that are too low or too high, giving easy passage only to frequencies within a certain range.</li>
<li>Band-pass filters can be made by stacking a low-pass filter on the end of a high-pass filter, or vice versa.</li>
<li>&ldquo;Attenuate&rdquo; means to reduce or diminish in amplitude. When you turn down the volume control on your stereo, you are &ldquo;attenuating&rdquo; the signal being sent to the speakers.</li>
</ul>
