# Introduction to Complex Numbers
<p>If I needed to describe the distance between two cities, I could provide an answer consisting of a single number in miles, kilometers, or some other unit of linear measurement.</p>
<p>However, if I were to describe how to travel from one city to another, I would have to provide more information than just the distance between those two cities; I would also have to provide information about the <em>direction</em> to travel, as well.</p>
<p>The kind of information that expresses a single dimension, such as linear distance, is called a <em>scalar</em> quantity in mathematics. Scalar numbers are the kind of numbers you&rsquo;ve used in most all of your mathematical applications so far.</p>
<p>The voltage produced by a battery, for example, is a scalar quantity. So is the<a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/textbook/direct-current/chpt-1/resistance/" target="_blank"> resistance</a> of a piece of wire (ohms), or the current through it (amps).</p>
<p>However, when we begin to analyze AC circuits, we find that quantity of<a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/video-lectures/electrical-quantities-a/" target="_blank"> voltage, current, and even resistance</a> (called <em>impedance</em> in AC) are not the familiar one-dimensional quantities we&rsquo;re used to measuring in DC circuits.</p>
<p>Rather, these quantities, because they&rsquo;re dynamic (alternating in direction and amplitude), possess other dimensions that must be taken into account. Frequency and phase shift are two of these dimensions that come into play.</p>
<p>Even with relatively simple AC circuits, where we&rsquo;re only dealing with a single frequency, we still have the dimension of phase shift to contend with in addition to the amplitude.</p>
<p>In order to successfully analyze AC circuits, we need to work with mathematical objects and techniques capable of representing these multi-dimensional quantities.</p>
<p>Here is where we need to abandon scalar numbers for something better suited: <em>complex numbers</em>. Just like the example of giving directions from one city to another, AC quantities in a single-frequency circuit have both amplitude (analogy: distance) and <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/textbook/alternating-current/chpt-1/ac-phase/" target="_blank">phase shift</a> (analogy: direction).</p>
<p>A complex number is a single mathematical quantity able to express these two dimensions of amplitude and phase shift at once.</p>
## Graphical Representation of Complex Numbers
<p>Complex numbers are easier to grasp when they&rsquo;re represented graphically. If I draw a line with a certain length (magnitude) and angle (direction), I have a graphic representation of a complex number which is commonly known in physics as a <em>vector</em>: (Figure below)</p>

<p>The idea of representing a number in graphical form is nothing new. We all learned this in grade school with the &ldquo;number line:&rdquo; (Figure below)</p>

<p>We even learned how addition and subtraction works by seeing how lengths (magnitudes) stacked up to give a final answer: (Figure below)</p>

<p>Later, we learned that there were ways to designate the values <em>between</em> the whole numbers marked on the line. These were fractional or decimal quantities: (Figure below)</p>

<p><img src="/uploads/articles/number-line-image3.png" /></p>

<p>These fields of numbers (whole, integer, rational, irrational, real, etc.) learned in grade school share a common trait: they&rsquo;re all <em>one-dimensional</em>. The straightness of the number line illustrates this graphically.</p>
<p>You can move up or down the number line, but all &ldquo;motion&rdquo; along that line is restricted to a single axis (horizontal).</p>
<p>One-dimensional, scalar numbers are perfectly adequate for counting beads, representing weight, or measuring DC battery voltage, but they fall short of being able to represent something more complex like the distance <em>and</em> direction between two cities, or the amplitude <em>and</em> phase of an AC waveform.</p>
<p>To represent these kinds of quantities, we need multidimensional representations. In other words, we need a number line that can point in different directions, and that&rsquo;s exactly what a vector is.</p>

<p><strong>REVIEW:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>A <em>scalar</em> number is the type of mathematical object that people are used to using in everyday life: a one-dimensional quantity like temperature, length, weight, etc.</li>
<li>A <em>complex number</em> is a mathematical quantity representing two dimensions of magnitude and direction.</li>
<li>A <em>vector</em> is a graphical representation of a complex number. It looks like an arrow, with a starting point, a tip, a definite length, and a definite direction. Sometimes the word <em>phasor</em> is used in electrical applications where the angle of the vector represents the phase shift between waveforms.</li>
</ul>
