<h1>AC Lab - Variable Inductor</h1>
<h3>Project Overview</h3>
<p>In this project, the construction of the simple <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/textbook/reference/chpt-9/inductors/" target="_blank">variable inductor circuit</a>, illustrated in Figure 1, will help you learn more about inductors.&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><img src="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/uploads/articles/variable-inductor-illustration.jpg" /></p>
<h5><em><strong>Figure 1.</strong> Variable inductor circuit.</em></h5>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>As you will experience, the placement of the steel bar affects the <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/textbook/direct-current/chpt-15/magnetic-fields-and-inductance/" target="_blank">inductance</a> of the wire coil and, therefore, the light through the lamp.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3>Parts and Materials</h3>
<ul>
<li>Paper tube from a toilet paper roll</li>
<li>Bar of iron or steel, large enough to almost fill the diameter of a paper tube</li>
<li>28 gauge magnet&nbsp;wire</li>
<li>Low-voltage AC power supply</li>
<li>Incandescent lamp, rated for power supply voltage</li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3>Learning Objectives</h3>
<ul>
<li>Determine the effects of <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/textbook/direct-current/chpt-14/permeability-and-saturation/" target="_blank">magnetic permeability</a> on inductance.</li>
<li>Determine how inductive reactance can control <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/textbook/direct-current/chpt-1/voltage-current/" target="_blank">current</a> in an AC circuit.</li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3>Instructions</h3>
<p><strong>Step 1:&nbsp;</strong>Wrap hundreds of turns of the magnet wire around the paper tube.</p>
<p><strong>Step 2: </strong>With the paper tube empty, connect this homemade inductor <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/textbook/direct-current/chpt-5/simple-series-circuits/" target="_blank">in series</a> with a low-voltage&nbsp;AC power supply and lamp to form a circuit, as illustrated in Figures 1 and 2.&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><img src="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/uploads/articles/variable-inductor-schematic-diagram.jpg" /></p>
<h5><em><strong>Figure 2.</strong> Variable inductor circuit schematic diagram</em></h5>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>You can use the low-voltage supply described in the earlier AC project, <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/textbook/experiments/chpt-4/transformer-power-supply/" target="_blank">Using a Transformer to Build a 12 VAC Power Supply</a>.&nbsp;When the tube is empty, the lamp should glow brightly.</p>
<p><strong>Step 3:&nbsp;</strong>Insert the steel bar into the tube. The&nbsp;lamp dims from the increased <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/textbook/direct-current/chpt-15/factors-affecting-inductance/" target="_blank">inductance</a> (L) and consequently increased inductive reactance (X<sub>L</sub>).</p>
<p><strong>Step 4:&nbsp;</strong>Try using bars of different materials, such as copper and stainless steel, if available. Not all metals have the same effect due to differences in magnetic permeability.</p>
