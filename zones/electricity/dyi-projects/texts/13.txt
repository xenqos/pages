# DC Lab - Introduction
<p>DC&nbsp;stands for <strong>d</strong>irect <strong>c</strong>urrent, in which there is a one-directional flow of electric charge. DC can be used to refer to both&nbsp;the voltage and the current in a DC circuit. These DC lab projects are designed to demonstrate&nbsp;important concepts of electricity related to DC circuits. These experiments complement the educational information available in the direct current textbook volume.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Each DC Lab project includes step-by-step instructions, a parts list, schematic diagrams, and&nbsp;illustrations of the completed projects similar to the example of&nbsp;Figure 1.</p>

<p><img src="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/uploads/articles/use-voltmeter-to-read-voltage-across-the-lamp-3.jpg" /></p>
<em><strong>Figure 1.</strong>&nbsp;Using an ammeter to measure current in a lamp circuit.</em>

<p>As shown in this figure, you will also learn how to use standard test equipment to measure current, voltage, and resistance.&nbsp;</p>
<p>The projects are designed to be relatively low-cost,&nbsp;educational, and even fun...like the potato battery experiment illustrated in Figure 2.&nbsp;</p>

<p><em><img src="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/uploads/articles/potato-battery-illustration.jpg" /></em></p>
<em><strong>Figure 2.</strong> Measuring the voltage generated by a potato battery.</em>

<p>Each project includes a list of the parts and materials you will need. These projects are great for educators, students, electronics hobbyists, makers, and anyone who wants to learn about DC electronics.</p>
