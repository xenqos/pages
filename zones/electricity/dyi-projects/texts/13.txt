# DC Lab - Introduction
<p>DC&nbsp;stands for <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/textbook/experiments/chpt-3/introduction-to-dc-circuits/" target="_blank"><strong>d</strong>irect <strong>c</strong>urrent</a>, in which there is a one-directional flow of electric charge. DC can be used to refer to both&nbsp;the <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/textbook/direct-current/chpt-1/voltage-current/" target="_blank">voltage and the current</a> in a DC circuit. These DC lab projects are designed to demonstrate&nbsp;important concepts of electricity related to DC circuits. These experiments complement the educational information available in the <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/textbook/direct-current/" target="_blank">direct current textbook volume</a>.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Each DC Lab project includes step-by-step instructions, a parts list, schematic diagrams, and&nbsp;illustrations of the completed projects similar to the example of&nbsp;Figure 1.</p>

<p><img src="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/uploads/articles/use-voltmeter-to-read-voltage-across-the-lamp-3.jpg" /></p>
#### <em><strong>Figure 1.</strong>&nbsp;Using an ammeter to measure current in a lamp circuit.</em>

<p>As shown in this figure, you will also learn how to use standard test equipment to measure current, voltage, and <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/textbook/direct-current/chpt-1/resistance/" target="_blank">resistance</a>.&nbsp;</p>
<p>The projects are designed to be relatively low-cost,&nbsp;educational, and even fun...like the <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/textbook/experiments/chpt-3/potato-battery/" target="_blank">potato battery experiment</a> illustrated in Figure 2.&nbsp;</p>

<p><em><img src="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/uploads/articles/potato-battery-illustration.jpg" /></em></p>
#### <em><strong>Figure 2.</strong> Measuring the voltage generated by a potato battery.</em>

<p>Each project includes a list of the parts and materials you will need. These projects are great for educators, students, electronics hobbyists, makers, and anyone who wants to learn about DC electronics.</p>
