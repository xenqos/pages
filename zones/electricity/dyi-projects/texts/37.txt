# AC Lab - Sensing AC Electric Fields
## Project Overview
<p>Low-level electric fields are all around us.&nbsp;These electric fields often interfere with other electronic systems through what is called electromagnetic interference (EMI).&nbsp;As illustrated in Figure 1, a simple wire may be used to sense&nbsp;AC electric&nbsp;fields using the audio detector circuit, explained in an earlier project chapter, to detect AC voltages in the audio frequencies.&nbsp;</p>

<p><img src="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/uploads/articles/sensing-ac-electric-fields-schematic-diagram.jpg" /></p>
<em><strong>Figure 1.</strong>&nbsp;The schematic diagram for sensing AC electric fields.</em>

## Parts and Materials
<ul>
<li>Audio detector with headphones</li>
</ul>

## Learning Objectives
<ul>
<li>To determine the effects of electrostatic (capacitive) coupling.</li>
<li>Electrostatic shielding techniques.</li>
</ul>

## Instructions
<p><strong>Step 1:&nbsp;</strong>Ground&nbsp;one lead of the detector to a metal object in contact with the earth (dirt); most any water pipe or faucet in a house will suffice. Take the other lead and hold it close to an electrical appliance or lamp fixture.</p>
<p><strong><span>WARNING:</span></strong>&nbsp;<strong>Do NOT try to make contact with the appliance or with any conductors within!</strong> Any AC electric fields produced by the appliance will be heard in the headphones as a buzzing tone.</p>

<p><img src="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/uploads/articles/sensing-ac-electric-fields-schematic-illustration.jpg" /></p>
<em><strong>Figure 2.</strong>&nbsp;Simplified illustration of the sensor system to detect AC electric fields.</em>

<p><strong>Step 2:&nbsp;</strong>Try holding the wire in different positions next to a good, strong source of electric fields.</p>
<p><strong>Step 3:&nbsp;</strong>Try using a piece of aluminum foil clipped to the wire&rsquo;s end to maximize capacitance (and, therefore, its ability to intercept an electric field).</p>
<p><strong>Step 4:&nbsp;</strong>Try using different types of material (paper, fabric, aluminum foil) to shield&nbsp;the wire from an electric field source. What material(s) works best? How does this compare with the AC magnetic field experiment?</p>
<p>As with magnetic fields, there is controversy about whether or not stray electric fields like these pose any health hazard to the human body.</p>
