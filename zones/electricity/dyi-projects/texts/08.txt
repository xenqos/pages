# Intro Lab - A Simple Lighting Circuit
## Project Overview
<p>In this project, you will build&nbsp;the simplest complete circuit in this collection of experiments: a battery and an incandescent lamp, as illustrated in Figure 1.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Many students assume that because electrons leave the negative (-) side of the battery and continue through the circuit to the positive (+) side. This is not true!&nbsp;</p>

### The Effects of a&nbsp;Short Circuit
<p>If you encounter a situation where the lamp refuses to light up, and the connecting wires are getting warm, you probably have a situation known as a short circuit, where a lower-resistance path than the lamp bypasses current around the lamp, preventing enough voltage from being dropped across the lamp to light it up. Figure 9 is&nbsp;is an example of a short circuit made on a breadboard.</p>

<p><img src="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/uploads/articles/short-circuit-case.jpg" /></p>
<em><strong>Figure 9.</strong>&nbsp;Example of a short circuit in the simple lamp circuit.</em>

<p>Figure 10 is an example of an accidental short circuit of the type typically made by students unfamiliar with breadboard usage.&nbsp;</p>

<p><img src="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/uploads/articles/example-of-an-accidental-short-circuit.jpg" /></p>
<em><strong>Figure 10.</strong>&nbsp;Example of an accidental short circuit on a breadboard.</em>

<p>Here there is no shorting&nbsp;wire present on the breadboard, yet there <em>is</em> a short circuit, and the lamp refuses to light.&nbsp;Based on your understanding of breadboard hole connections, can you determine where the short&nbsp;is in this circuit?</p>

### Tips to Avoid Short Circuits
<p>Short circuits are generally to be avoided, as they result in very high current flow rates, which cause&nbsp;wires to heat up and battery power sources to deplete.&nbsp;If the power source is substantial enough, a short circuit may also cause heat of explosive proportions to manifest, thus creating&nbsp;equipment damage and hazard to nearby personnel.&nbsp;This is what happens when a tree limb shorts&nbsp;across wires on a power line;&nbsp;the limb&mdash;being composed of wet wood&mdash;acts as a low-resistance path to electric current, resulting in heat and sparks.</p>
