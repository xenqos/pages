<h1>Si Lab - Simple Op Amp</h1>
<h3>Project Overview</h3>
<p>This circuit design improves on the previous&nbsp;<a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/textbook/experiments/chpt-5/differential-amplifier/" target="_blank">differential amplifier circuit project</a>.&nbsp;As illustrated in Figure 1, there are two primary advantages of this new circuit:</p>
<ol>
<li>A <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/textbook/semiconductors/chpt-4/current-mirrors/" target="_blank">current mirror</a> comprised of NPN transistors, Q<sub>5</sub> and Q<sub>6,</sub>&nbsp;is used to&nbsp;improve&nbsp;the bias current stability to the differential pair, Q<sub>3</sub> and Q<sub>4</sub>. This replaces a simple <a href="https://eepower.com/resistor-guide/" target="_blank">resistor</a>.</li>
<li>A second current mirror using&nbsp;<a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/technical-articles/pnp-transistor-characteristics-applications/" target="_blank">PNP transistors</a>, Q<sub>1</sub> and Q<sub>2</sub>,&nbsp;replaces&nbsp;the load resistor. The high <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/textbook/alternating-current/chpt-5/series-r-l-and-c/" target="_blank">impedance</a> of this current mirror increases the voltage gain of the <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/textbook/semiconductors/chpt-1/amplifiers/" target="_blank">amplifier</a>.</li>
</ol>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><img src="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/uploads/articles/simple-op-amp-schematic-diagram.jpg" /></p>
<h5><em><strong>Figure 1.</strong> Schematic diagram of an op amp with differential inputs and current mirror load.</em></h5>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>With a higher voltage gain, this circuit is able to function as a working <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/textbook/semiconductors/chpt-8/introduction-operational-amplifiers/" target="_blank">operational amplifier or op amp</a>. Op amps form the basis of a great many modern analog semiconductor circuits, so understanding the internal workings of an op amp&nbsp;is important. The PNP transistors, Q<sub>1</sub> and Q<sub>2</sub>, form a current mirror that tries to keep the current split equally through the two differential pair transistors, Q<sub>3</sub> and Q<sub>4</sub>. NPN transistors, Q<sub>5</sub> and Q<sub>6</sub>, form another current mirror, setting the total differential pair current at a level predetermined by resistor R<sub>prg</sub>.</p>

<h4>Using the Op Amp in a Noninverting Amplifier Circuit</h4>
<p>A more complex op amp circuit is called the <a href="https://www.allaboutcircuits.com/textbook/experiments/chpt-6/noninverting-amplifier/" target="_blank">noninverting amplifier</a>, and it uses a pair of resistors in the feedback loop to feedback&nbsp;a fraction of the output voltage to the inverting input, causing the amplifier to output a voltage equal to some multiple of the voltage at the non-inverting input.</p>

<p>If we use two equal-value resistors, the feedback voltage will be 1/2 the output voltage, causing the output voltage to become twice the voltage impressed at the noninverting input. Thus, we have a voltage amplifier with a precise gain of two.</p>
<p><strong>Step 11: </strong>Measure the input and output voltages as you vary the potentiometer. As you test this noninverting amplifier circuit, you may notice slight discrepancies between the output and input voltages.</p>
<p>According to the feedback resistor values, the voltage gain should be exactly two.&nbsp;However, you may notice deviations in the order of several hundredths of a volt between what the output voltage is and what it should be. These deviations are due to imperfections in the differential amplifier circuit and may be greatly diminished if we add more amplification stages to increase the differential voltage gain.</p>

<h4>Changing the Bias Current of the Differential Input Transistor Pair</h4>
<p><strong>Step 12:&nbsp;</strong>One way we can maximize the precision of the existing circuit is to change the resistance of R<sub>prg</sub>. This resistor sets the lower current mirror&rsquo;s control point and, in so doing, influences many performance parameters of the op amp.&nbsp;Try substituting difference resistance values, ranging from 10 k&Omega; to 1 M&Omega;. Do not use a resistance less than 10 k&Omega;, or else the current mirror transistors may begin to overheat and thermally run away.</p>
<p>Some operational amplifiers available in prepackaged units provide a way for the user to similarly program&nbsp;the differential pair&rsquo;s current mirror and are called programmable op amps. Most op amps are not programmable and have their internal current mirror control points fixed by internal resistance, trimmed to precise value at the factory.</p>
